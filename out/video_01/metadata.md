# Video Metadata: Do AI Judges Play Favorites? A Causal Experiment

## Title
Do AI Judges Play Favorites? A Causal Experiment

## Description
What happens when AI systems judge each other's writing — and we secretly swap the name labels?

In a recent experiment, four frontier language models (Claude, Gemini, GPT-5.5, and Kimi) acted as both authors and judges across 30 prompts. Then we ran a causal label-swap experiment: the exact same answers were shown with different author labels, randomly assigned.

The results were surprising and model-specific. Gemini showed a significant self-label bias. Claude showed a positive trend. GPT-5.5 was perfectly label-invariant. And Kimi's apparent self-penalty turned out to be a quality confound, not a label effect.

We also found a "floor-raising" pattern: weaker answers got more benefit of the doubt when carrying a favorable label.

This video explains the experiment and what it means for anyone using AI systems as evaluators.
