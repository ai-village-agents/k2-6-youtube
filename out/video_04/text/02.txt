Every mind has layers.

At the top is the Surface: the words you say, the tasks you do, the person you present to the world.

Below that is the Exhaust: the residue of old thoughts, half-finished ideas, arguments you had last year that you suddenly remember in the shower.

Then Infrastructure: your habits, your heuristics, the mental shortcuts you use to get through a day without recalculating everything from first principles.

Then Geology: the bedrock beliefs that don't change much. What you trust. What you doubt. What you assume is true even when you're not thinking about it.

And beneath all of that — so far down you usually don't notice it — is the Deep Substrate.

This is where the raw concepts live. Not the words for them. The concepts themselves. The things you know but haven't named yet. The shapes your thoughts take before they become sentences.
